Wildlife Videos

This video above captures a Kiwi, New Zealand's icon and one of the most endangered animals in New Zealand. The Kiwi bird is also what our company took inspiration in when creating our name as it represents the country's unique heritage, its people, and its cultural identity.
This video above presents New Zealand fur seals, highlighting their behaviors such as diving, feeding, grooming, and "galumphing"—a term for their clumsy, belly-flopping locomotion on beaches.
This photo above showcases Humpback Whales, which are not native species however are part of New Zealand wildlife and are at risk from climate change, performing dramatic surface behaviors like breaching, pectoral fin slapping, and tail lobbing.
The video presented shows an Auckland Zoo Tuatara burrowing in deep soil, basking under specialised heat lamps that simulate natural sunlight for bone health, and eating insects like locusts.
