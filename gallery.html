Wildlife Photos

This photo above captures a Kiwi, New Zealand's icon and one of the most endangered animals in New Zealand. The Kiwi bird is also what our company took inspiration in when creating our name as it represents the country's unique heritage, its people, and its cultural identity.
The picture above shows the New Zealand fur seal, Kekeno, with thousands residing along the rocky coastline, particularly at the Kaikoura Peninsula. Although this species is not currently endangered, they are experiencing the direct consequences of climate change, particularly warming oceans and a decline in prey.
Shown above is the kea bird, another one of New Zealand's endangered species where its population is listed at approximately 3000-7000 individuals. The Kea bird is also one of the most intelligent birds in the world, often used to represent problem-solving and creativity.
This photo shows a Tuatara, a species that coexisted with dinosaurs 200 million years ago, capture its prey. Tuatara once lived throughout mainland Aotearoa however due to poaching and habitat destruction, they are extremely vulnerable to extinction.
The Humpback Whale, which can sometimes rarely be seen at beaches or coasts of New Zealand, are again one of the species in New Zealand that are vulnerable to the effects of climate change, as well as human-caused threats such as entanglement.
Below shows a PekaPeka bat lowering at a lake to catch its prey at daytime. Bats are actually New Zealand's only native land mammals, and additionally "Nationally Critical" meaning they are in danger of extinction if we do not protect the.
